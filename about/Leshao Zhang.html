Leshao Zhang

Ph.D. in cognitive science with the background in computer science and design. Experienced in computer programming and algorithm design. He was employed by Queen Mary University of London and GoldSmiths, University of London as Teaching Assistant and Associate Lecturer for over one year for the courses of Graphic User Interface, Design for Human Interaction, Software and Network Service Design, Multimedia Techniques, Web Programming for undergraduates and postgraduates. Part of his research has been published at well know conferences and journals and has good impact in its field.

Keywords

Human Computer Interaction, Human Interaction, Virtual Reality, User Experience Research.

Research Interests

His research focused on human (computer) interaction, nonverbal interaction in Virtual Reality and consumer behavior in the Immersive Virtual Environment. He is experienced in big data video analysis, motion capture techniques as well as hardware interaction. His Ph.D. project is about non-verbal Interaction in Virtual Reality. It looked into how people’s nonverbal behavior affects the people’s preferences in the Virtual Reality. Vicon motion capture system was used to support participant’s embodied experience as well as collecting movement data for analysis. Complex dynamics analysis was used in the research.
